This is a study by the Max Planck Institute for Research on Collective Goods in Germany. The aim is to understand the processes underlying evaluations of narratives.

Can I participate?

Anyone who can complete the study without wearing glasses and is between 18 and 35 years old can participate. The study is conducted in English, and you may participate if you have a good understanding of the language. You will need a computer (laptop or desktop, no tablets or phones) with a webcam and audio output to take part.

What can I expect if I participate in this study?

The study takes approximately 20 minutes. For your participation, you will receive a payment of 3 GBP.

General Information

You will be asked questions about personal attitudes, evaluations, and demographic information. However, we do not ask for your name or sensitive personal data. All information you provide is completely anonymous. Your data will be randomly assigned a participant number by the system.

Eye-Tracking

This study uses your webcam for eye-tracking. The recorded data shows where you are looking on your screen. NO video of you will be sent to us. The data consists only of two numbers indicating the position of your gaze on the screen. We do not record your screen or any video material of you.

To participate, you must consent to the use of your camera on the next screen. If you do not wish for the camera to be used, please close this page.

What should I know about my participation in this study?

  • Your participation is voluntary.
  • To our knowledge, there are no risks associated with participating in this study.
  • You may end your participation at any time.
  • Your decisions will not be used against you.
  • There are no negative consequences if you choose to withdraw from participation.
  • To ensure transparency of scientific findings, the fully anonymized dataset will be published and made available to other researchers. Please note that the potential purposes of future analyses of the data cannot be predicted.

Who can I contact?

If you have any questions, concerns, or complaints, or believe that you have suffered harm as a result of this study, please contact Dr. Rima-Maria Rahal via rahal@coll.mpg.de.
